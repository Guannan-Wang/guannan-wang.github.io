## About

I am a Postdoctoral Associate in the laboratory of José Dinneny at Stanford University and the Howard Hughes Medical Institute. I earned my Ph.D. in Biological Sciences from Louisiana State University, working with Dr. Maheshi Dassanayake. My research investigates how extremophytes — plants that thrive under environmental extremes such as high salinity, boron toxicity, drought, and temperature stress — have evolved diverse strategies for stress tolerance. Using the mustard family Brassicaceae as a natural experiment in evolution, I combine comparative multi-omics, single-cell transcriptomics, functional genetics, and deep-learning approaches for cross-species comparison to identify the innovations that allow some species to tolerate stresses that their close relatives cannot. Ultimately, I wish to translate these naturally selected solutions into climate-resilient crops.
